Wiping over all of the head as Allaah Ta'ala says:

وَامْسَحُوا بِرُءُوسِكُمْ

rub your heads (with water)

Ibn Qudaamah says in 'al-Mughnee':

Some of the people who claim that that which is ordered to be wiped is part of the head only - claim that the ب [in the verse] means "part of" as if He had said: "وَامْسَحُوا بَعْضَ رُءُوسِكُمْ". However, we say that in His saying "وَامْسَحُوا بِرُءُوسِكُمْ" the ب is as if He said regarding Tayammum "فَامْسَحُوا بِوُجُوهِكُمْ."

Ibn Burhaan said: "He who claims that the ب means "part of" has declared before the scholars of the language that of which they have no knowledge."

In the hadeeth of'Abdullaah ibn Zayd is a further proof of the incorrectness of the saying that it means "a part of":

أَنَّ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم مَسَحَ رَأْسَهُ بِيَدَيْهِ فَأَقْبَلَ بِهِمَا وَأَدْبَرَ بَدَأَ بِمُقَدَّمِ رَأْسِهِ ثُمَّ ذَهَبَ بِهِمَا إِلَى قَفَاهُ ثُمَّ رَدَّهُمَا حَتَّى رَجَعَ إِلَى الْمَكَانِ الَّذِي بَدَأَ مِنْهُ

The Prophet pbuh wiped his head with his two hands, moving them forwards and backwards - beginning with the front of the head and (wiping) with them up to his nape then he pbuh returned them to the place from which he began.

The ruling for the ears is the same as that for the head. It is authentically reported that Rasoolullaah pbuh said:

الأُذُنَانِ مِنَ الرَّأْسِ

The two ears are a part of the head.
