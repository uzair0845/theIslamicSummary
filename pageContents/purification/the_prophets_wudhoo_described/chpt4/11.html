The arms here which we are ordered to wash begin with the fingertips and hands - which are to be included in this washing - they being part of the arm (yad) which we are ordered to Wash in the aayah.

Humraan ibn Abaan reports that 'Uthmaan (radhi-yAllaahu 'anhu) called for water for wudhoo and mentioned the Prophet's (pbuh) way of making wudhoo - and Humraan said:

ثُمَّ غَسَلَ يَدَهُ الْيُمْنَى إِلَى الْمِرْفَقِ ثَلاَثَ مَرَّاتٍ ثُمَّ غَسَلَ يَدَهُ الْيُسْرَى مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ

Then he washed his right arm including the elbow three times, then the left in the same way.

Nu'aym ibn Mijmar said:

رَأَيْتُ أَبَا هُرَيْرَةَ يَتَوَضَّأُ فَغَسَلَ وَجْهَهُ فَأَسْبَغَ الْوُضُوءَ ثُمَّ غَسَلَ يَدَهُ الْيُمْنَى حَتَّى أَشْرَعَ فِي الْعَضُدِ ثُمَّ يَدَهُ الْيُسْرَى حَتَّى أَشْرَعَ فِي الْعَضُدِ

He washed his face and washed it well. He then washed his right hand including a portion of his arm. He then washed his left hand including a portion of his arm.

In the end of the hadeeth he said:

رَأَيْتُ رَسُولَ اللَّهِ صلى الله عليه وسلم يَتَوَضَّأُ

This is how I saw Allah's Messenger (pbuh) perform his ablution.
