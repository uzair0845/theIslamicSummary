Allaah Ta'ala says:

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا قُمْتُمْ إِلَى الصَّلَاةِ فَاغْسِلُوا وُجُوهَكُمْ وَأَيْدِيَكُمْ إِلَى الْمَرَافِقِ وَامْسَحُوا بِرُءُوسِكُمْ وَأَرْجُلَكُمْ إِلَى الْكَعْبَيْنِ

O Ye who believe! When ye prepare for prayer, wash your faces, and your hands (and arms) to the elbows; rub your heads (with water); and (wash) your feet to the ankles

Abu Hurayrah (radhi-yAllaahu 'anhu) said: Rasoolullaah pbuh said:

لاَ تُقْبَلُ صَلاَةُ أَحَدِكُمْ إِذَا أَحْدَثَ حَتَّى يَتَوَضَّأَ

The Prayer of anyone of you breaching purification is not accepted unless he makes wudhoo.

Ibn 'Abbaas (radhi-yAllaahu 'anhumaa) said: Rasoolullaah pbuh said:

إِنَّمَا أُمِرْتُ بِالْوُضُوءِ إِذَا قُمْتُ إِلَى الصَّلاَةِ

Verily I have been ordered to make wudhoo when I stand for Prayer.

Abu Sa'eed (radhi-yAllaahu 'anhu) said: Rasoolullaah pbuh said:

مِفْتَاحُ الصَّلاَةِ الطُّهُورُ وَتَحْرِيمُهَا التَّكْبِيرُ وَتَحْلِيلُهَا التَّسْلِيمُ

The key to Prayer is purification, its 'tahreem' is the takbeer and its 'tahleel' is the giving salaam.

  1. Sahih Muslim (225)
  2. Sunan Abi Dawood (3760)
  3. That which makes normal actions, such as eating and talking, forbidden therein. again
  4. That which renders normal actions permissible.
  5. Ibn Maajah (Book 1, Hadith 289)
