Basmalah Aloud In The Prayer

As for Basmalah aloud during the prayer, those who did not agree that it is a part of Al-Fatihah, state that the Basmalah should not be aloud.

The scholars who stated that Bismillah is a part of every Surah (except chapter 9) had different opinions; some of them, such as Ash-Shafi'i, said that one should recite Bismillah with Al-Fatihah aloud. This is also the opinion of many among the Companions, the Tabi'in and the Imams of Muslims from the Salaf and the later generations. The proof that these scholars relied on is that, since Bismillah is a part of Al-Fatihah, it should be recited aloud like the rest of Al-Fatihah. Also, An-Nasa'i recorded in his Sunan, that Abu Hurayrah once performed the prayer and recited Bismillah aloud, and after he finished the prayer, he said, "Among you, I perform the prayer that is the closest to the prayer of the Messenger of Allah pbuh."

In Sahih Al-Bukhari it is recorded that Anas bin Malik was asked about the recitation of the Prophet pbuh. He said, "His recitation was unhurried." He then demonstrated that and recited, while lengthening the recitation of Bismillah Ar-Rahman Ar-Rahim.
