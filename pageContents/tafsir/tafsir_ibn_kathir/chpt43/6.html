Ibrahim's Declaration Of Tawhid Here

He disowned his father's and his people's worship of idols:

وَإِذْ قَالَ إِبْرَهِيمُ لاًّبِيهِ وَقَوْمِهِ إِنَّنِى بَرَآءٌ مِّمَّا تَعْبُدُونَ - إِلاَّ الَّذِى فَطَرَنِى فَإِنَّهُ سَيَهْدِينِ وَجَعَلَهَا كَلِمَةً بَـقِيَةً فِى عَقِبِهِ

"Verily, I am innocent of what you worship, except Him Who created me; and verily, He will guide me." And he made it a Word lasting among his offspring.

Ikrimah, Mujahid, Ad-Dahhak, Qatadah, As-Suddi and others commented on the Ayah:

وَجَعَلَهَا كَلِمَةً بَـقِيَةً فِى عَقِبِهِ

This means, La ilaha illallah, and there are still those among his offspring who say it.

A similar view was narrated from Ibn 'Abbas. Ibn Zayd said, "The word of Islam."
